Nutrition

When you are prepare correctly you can perform at your peak and when you recover correctly you can do it all again. This will help you increase you fitness and eliminate fatigue before and after games. This also gives routine and focus before exercise. Don't exercise without eating correctly. You will not get the benefit from your efforts and you will not do yourself and the team justice.

Match / training day: Your body needs fuel!

1. Plenty of liquids. Sip Sip Sip! Make it a habit. If you are dehydrated your body and brain function deteriorates. Don't just gulp down a lot of water before the game you should drink a little and often. No caffeine

2. Eat a substantial meal a minimum of 3 hours before the match/training. Plenty of slow release carbohydrates. Potatoes, brown bread, brown rice.

3. 30 minutes before training a jam sandwich on white bread , an energy bar or jellies, An Isotonic drink. (You can make your own isotonic drink: A quarter tea spoon of salt 100ml Mi Wadi and to 400 water. This loads your muscles with glycogen. You will burn this glycogen during the match.

3. Half time isotonic drink and jellies. This replaces glycogen in the muscles and speeds up recovery.

4. Immediately in after the match, before togging in , isotonic drink, jellies banana.

5. Eat a substantial meal within 1 hour of the match/training. Plenty of protein. Fish, chicken. Protein is the raw material to repair muscles and this will speed up recovery.

Food Pyramid

To make it easier to see what you should be including in your diet to make it healthy use the food pyramid shown below. The idea is to eat more foods from the shelves which are larger and less from those which are smaller.

TRUE: Teenagers need extra calcium and protein as their bodies are growing so much

FALSE: Breads, cereals, potatoes, pasta, and rice are high carbohydrate, low fat foods. It's what we do to them that effects the fat content e.g. make chips out of potatoes, add rich creamy sauces to pasta etc.

FALSE: Low fat milk contains the same amount of calcium as full fat. Calcium is important for strong bones and teeth.

FALSE: We need to drink 8 cups of fluid every day.

TRUE: Iron helps to carry oxygen in our blood from our lungs to every body cell. A lack of iron can lead to anaemia and tiredness. Vitamin C, found in fruit and some vegetables helps your body to absorb the iron.

FALSE: The sugar in chocolate is absorbed quickly into the blood, which gives you a short energy boost, followed by a sharp dip, when you'll feel hungry again. It takes longer for your body to break down bread, so you get a steady release of energy for a longer length of time.

TRUE: An average bag of crisps contains 0.8g salt, while 2 medium slices of bread contains 0.83g salt. Most of the salt in our diets comes from processed foods, including processed meats e.g. bacon and bread, soups, sauces and breakfast cereals. However, bread and breakfast cereals are nutritious foods and should be included as part of a healthy balanced diet.

TRUE: Brown bread contains much more fibre than white bread. The body can't digest fibre so it doesn't provide us with energy but it does help to move waste along the digestive system. It helps protect us against constipation and other bowel disorders, certain cancers and heart disease.

TRUE: Unsalted popcorn is a great alternative to a packet of crisps. It's much lower in fat and salt but still gives the same crunch!

FALSE: It takes about 20 minutes for the brain to get the message that we are full. To avoid that 'stuffed feeling', chew your food well and take time to taste and enjoy it with family and friends, rather than speeding through your meal in front of the TV.
