Profiles

On this page we feature female sport role models. After reading these articles and watching these clips I have no excuse not to get up off my bum and get involved in some physical activity!

Imagine all your dreams for a sport you are passionate about being wiped out in one day - what would you do? Well before you answer take a look at the section on Bethany Hamilton.

Do you moan about going to training on a rainy day or missing out on other activities because of your commitments to sport? Check out the section on Grainne Murphy and think about the sacrifices she made to get to the Olympics - it might inspire you to keep up the training!!

If that doesn't motivate you, take a look at the section on Evanne Ni Chuillin who has continued to participate in sport as an adult and made a successful career out of her interest in sport.

And what about Katie Taylor? She's probably the greatest athlete this country has ever produced. Furthermore she has really put women's boxing on the map and is an ambassador second to none for her chosen sport. She seems really down-to-earth and humble and is still happy to train in very basic conditions. Check out the youtube link to the Sky interview with her and to the songs she has inspired as she goes for gold in the Olympics this summer!

Katie Taylor

Katie Taylor from Bray, Co. Wicklow began boxing in 1998. She first made Irish boxing history at age 15, on Oct 31 2001 in the first officially sanctioned women's bout ever held in Ireland, a momentous day for Irish boxing. She has gone on to win 9 European and 4 World Championships. She is the top boxer in the world in her weight category and a great ambassador for women's boxing in Ireland.

Katie's impressive athletic accomplishments are not limited to boxing - she is also captained the Irish Women's soccer team!

There are very few people in the world who have participated so successfully in two different sports at the highest level and it shows what a remarkable person Katie is. She has shown that it is possible for Irish women to conquer the world.

Evanne Ni Chuillin

Sports mad, Evanne has played camogie since she was a child and still plays as an adult with Portebello Camogie Club. The 29 year old has made a successful career as one of Ireland's leading sports journalists with a regular slot on the Six One News on RTE. She has made her mark in a very male-dominated career of sports coverage and brings glamour to sport as demonstrated by the Twitter response to her low-cut blue dress last November!

Bethany Hamilton

Bethany Hamilton survived a shark attack when she was just 13 years old. She lost her left arm in this attack which seemed to ruin a promising career in professional surfing. However Bethany's courage and determination helped her to teach herself to surf again and to pursue a professional surfing career.

Grainne Murphy

19 year old Grainne Murphy, originally from Wexford, moved to Limerick when she was 13 years old in order to be close to the Olympic standard training facilities at University of Limerick Arena. Despite moving home and away from all her friends at such a young age because of her commitment to sport she says "I love what I do so I wouldn't really call it a sacrifice". She always had the London 2012 Olympics as a goal.

Her hard work has paid off because she has gone from provincial and national competitions to Europe and World events and now she will make her first appearance at the Olympics this summer to compete in the 800m freestyle.
