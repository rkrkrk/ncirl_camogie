Exercise to stay fit and healthy

Over half of primary school age children do not achieve the recommended level of physical activity. By 15 years of age, almost nine out of 10 girls and seven out of 10 boys don't achieve the recommended level. One in five Irish children and teenagers is overweight or obese.

Although many people think of health in terms of illness, health is a positive concept that covers your physical, mental and social well-being . Exercise benefits every aspect of your health.

To be a healthy weight, you need to take regular physical activity - at least 60 minutes per day on average - and eat a healthy diet. This ensures a balance between the energy you get from food and the energy you use.

Teenagers should be active, at a moderate to vigorous level, for at least 60 minutes every day.

Conditioning Exercises

In addition to regular camogie training you should carry out the following exercises 2 to 3 times per week. This will help build core strength and flexibility. Warm up for 10 minutes before doing the exercises. Do the full set of exercises twice. Make sure you have done the exercises at training with a coach first to make sure your technique is correct.

Hip extension

Hold extension for 3 seconds, 10 repetitions

Cat Stretch

8 repetitions, hold stretches for 3 seconds

Squat

10 repetitions

Bridge Raise

Hold for 3 seconds, 10 repetitions

Side PLank

Alternate sides, hold for at least 15 seconds

Plank

Hold for 20 secs, try and work up to 40

Side Lunge

10 repetitions, alternate to each side

Lunge

10 repetitions, alternate each foot

Caterpiller Walk

8 repetitions
